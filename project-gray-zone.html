Project Gray Zone Project Gray Zone

Project Gray Zone是Kalyna OSINT于2026年2月12日启动的项目。灰色地带概念已经随战争形态变化而发生偏移，而我们的目标是更新公众对“灰色地带”的理解框架，推动OSINT社区转变在开源分析中对“灰色地带”的定义。本页将持续汇总定义、口径、案例与来源。

Project Gray Zone is a Kalyna OSINT initiative launched on February 12, 2026. As the concept of “gray zones” has shifted with changes in the character of war, our goal is to update the public framework for understanding them and to encourage the OSINT community to refine how “gray zones” are defined in open-source analysis. This page will be continuously maintained as a living reference, consolidating definitions, analytic criteria, cases, and sources.

该分析将随着研究和更多数据而持续更新，你所看到的并不是最终内容。你可以从Kalyna OSINT官方网站“项目”部分再次进入。

This analysis will be updated as research progresses and additional data becomes available; what you are reading is not the final version. You can revisit the latest iteration via the “Projects” section on the official Kalyna OSINT website.

新的地图参考标准 New Mapping Reference Standard

随着我们更新对灰色地带的定义，我们的互动地图Kalyna Battle Map所绘制的前线也将参照这一标准发生改变。目前，我们将在第聂伯彼得罗浮斯克和扎波罗热地区率先使用这一标准。

As we revise our definition of gray zones, the front line depicted on our interactive map (Kalyna Battle Map) will be updated accordingly. We will apply this standard first in Dnipropetrovsk and Zaporizhzhia regions.

新的前线取消了灰色地带，只由红蓝控制区和它们重叠的部分构成，下文将详细解释这样做的含义。

Under the new approach, the “gray zone” layer is removed. The front line will consist only of red and blue control areas and the portions where these areas overlap. The implications of this change are explained below.

灰色地带在过去的定义 Previous Definitions of the Gray Zone

在过去，特别是2023-2024年间，战争研究机构和开源分析社区对于灰色地带的定义主要是两种区域的叠加或其中一种：灰色地带和交战区。灰色地带代表着一片不存在战争双方阵地的区域；而交战区则代表着一片双方士兵都能临时进入并接触，但难以站稳脚跟的区域。

In earlier periods—especially across 2023–2024—research institutions and the OSINT community commonly used two related notions: a “gray zone” and a “contested zone.” The gray zone referred to areas where neither side held established positions, while the contested zone referred to areas where forces could temporarily enter and make contact but could not reliably consolidate control.

相对的，双方都存在稳定的控制线，在控制线内友军士兵占据所有阵地，控制所有增援路线，形成对区域的稳定控制，几乎无需担心存在敌方渗透。因此，在那一段时间通过地理定位来证实前线变化是十分容易的，受到对方攻击的定位代表友方稳定控制，受到友方攻击的定位代表敌方稳定控制，双方袭击频繁的区域则为交战区。

By contrast, both sides could maintain relatively stable control lines. Within these lines, friendly forces held positions and controlled reinforcement routes, leaving limited room for deep infiltration. As a result, geolocation-based verification of front-line changes was often straightforward: strikes attributed to the enemy typically indicated friendly control, strikes attributed to friendly forces often indicated enemy control, and areas with frequent mutual strikes were treated as contested.

现在的“灰色地带” What “Gray Zone” Means Today

在2025年间，由于缺少真正的突破力量、实现目标所需要的人力和迫于宣传需要，俄罗斯军队大规模通过自杀式袭击或渗透的方式将步兵送入目标地点展示旗帜。这些士兵通过上述手段进入到目标地点并藏匿，他们无法得到来自稳定控制区的增援和补给，也难以仅凭自己站稳脚跟，他们的唯一任务是生存下去，而不是交战，甚至在一些情况下最好避免交战。

In 2025, limited breakthrough capacity, manpower constraints, and propaganda pressures led Russian forces to insert infantry into target locations at scale—often through high-risk assaults or infiltration intended to “show the flag.” After entering, these troops frequently concealed themselves without reliable reinforcement or resupply from stable control areas. In many cases, they could not realistically consolidate control on their own; their primary objective became survival rather than sustained combat—and sometimes avoiding contact altogether.

这是我们有关俄军第一次渗透波克罗夫斯克的报告，以便你可以更好的理解上文所描述的现状。这种情况在几乎所有前线发生，相信大部分人都已有所耳闻。

For additional context, see our report on the Russian forces’ first infiltration into Pokrovsk, which illustrates the dynamics described above. Variations of this pattern have appeared across much of the front, and many observers have already encountered similar accounts.

不仅如此，恶劣的天气和无人机的大量使用也让步兵渗透更加容易。

Moreover, adverse weather and the widespread use of drones have further altered the tactical environment, often making infantry infiltration easier than before.
